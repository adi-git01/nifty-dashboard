1. Deep Industry Cycles & Price Lag (The Lead/Lag Playbook)

We mapped the Nifty 500 to granular Screener industries over 15 years to study cycle length and price vs earnings lag. The findings completely tear down the myth that "price always leads earnings by 6 months." It's highly industry-specific.

The Price vs Earnings Lag Playbook

Dynamic Industries (Strategy: How to play this)
Price LEADS Earnings (by 6 mo) IT Software, Realty, Insurance, Transport Infra
(Play: Buy on orderbook/guidance, sell before earnings actually peak)
Price LEADS Earnings (by 2 mo) Textiles, Diversified, Media, Gas, Auto Components
(Play: Buy the breakout, the earnings will follow next quarter)
Price = Earnings (CONCURRENT) Capital Markets, Banks, Metals, Automobiles, Aerospace, Power
(Play: Trend following. Price and earnings peak at the exact same time. No predictive edge from earnings.)

[!TIP] In Capital Markets, Banks, and Metals, do not try to front-run the cycle. The market does not look ahead in these sectors; it prices the exact present. By contrast, in IT and Realty, the market starts selling off 6 months before earnings actually decline.

Cycle Lengths & Amplitude (Trough to Trough)

How long does a sector run before you need to re-evaluate? * Fastest Cycles (1.5 years): Insurance (15.8mo), Ag & Commercial Vehicles (18mo), Transport Services (19mo) * Mid Cycles (2 years): Leisure/Hotels (23mo), Realty (23mo), Consumer Durables (20mo) * Long Cycles (3 years): IT Software (36mo), Textiles (34mo) — These yield the highest amplitude moves (Textiles +600%, IT +250%).

Bear Market Protectors (Granular Level)

If the Nifty drops, these granular industries historically generate massive positive alpha: 1. Agri Food & Other Products (+2.5% alpha per month in bears) 2. IT Services (+2.4% alpha per month) 3. Pharmaceuticals / Biotech (+1.6% alpha per month) 4. Food Products (+1.1% alpha per month)

2. The Cost of Being "Early" in Value (QVR Backtests)

We built and backtested a "Quality Value Recovery" (QVR) strategy using Mean Reversion logic (Quality>=6, Value>=6, Trend 40-65 recovering). We tested two variants against DNA3-V3.1 (pure momentum):

  1. QVR-Fusion (QVR Entry + V3.1 trailing exits): +5.5% CAGR vs V3.1's +17.4%
  2. QVR-Swing (QVR Entry + Hard +20% profit target / 60-day time stop): +4.1% CAGR

Key Finding: Value without Momentum is Dead Money. In the QVR-Swing test, the strategy timed out (held for 60 days without hitting +20% or -10%) 53.5% of the time. It only hit the +20% target 18% of the time. Conclusion: Buying beaten-down high-quality companies before strong momentum is confirmed means holding dead money for months while the rest of the market rallies. DNA3-V3.1's approach (wait for the uptrend, pay up for momentum) vastly outperforms mean reversion.

3. Structural Shifts: The COVID Effect on Lags

We analyzed the lead/lag correlation over different epochs (Pre-COVID 2010-2019 vs Post-COVID 2020-2026) to see if markets have fundamentally changed. Key Finding: The market has become significantly MORE forward-looking post-COVID. * Banks: Pre-COVID price led earnings by 3 months. Post-COVID, price leads by 6 months. * IT Services: Pre-COVID was concurrent. Post-COVID, price leads by 3 months. * Consumer Durables: Pre-COVID price led by 1 month. Post-COVID, price leads by 4 months. * Media & Textiles: Both went from concurrent to Price Leading by 2 months.

[!IMPORTANT] Because retail and institutional participation increased massively post-COVID, information is priced in much faster. You must buy anticipating the cycle earlier now than you had to in 2015.
